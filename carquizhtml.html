Car Performance

Cars are a great mode of transportation. They can lessen the time it takes from going from point A to Point B depending on the speed. Some people increase the power of their car to make going from point to point a lot faster. There are many different combinations of engine parts that can increase the power of a car, but there are only a few serious power adders.

The Turbo

A turbo charger is used in many production cars and has many other automotive and machinery applications. An engine is basically just an mechanized pump; it needs air and fuel to work and create power. The more air and fuel and engine can take in the more power it can create. A turbo can increase the amount of air an engine can take in drastically. Using an engines already existing exhaust gasses, a turbos turbine spins at speeds over 5000RPM (dependant on the turbo). This in turn creates mechanical energy which drives a turbos compressor which feeds cooled and compressed air into an engines cylinders. Increasing the air in the cylinders allows more kinetic energy (in the form of explosions) inside the cylinders increasing the power output. Since a turbo runs off of exhaust gasses there is some lag between the time a turbo is at its starting RPM to its highest RPM.

The Supercharger

A supercharger has the same goal as a turbo; to increase the amount of air available to an engines cylinders. The main difference is that a supercharger is driven by the same belt that powers a cars A/C, Powersteering, and Alternator. An advantage to using a supercharger is that it is running off a cars serpentine belt to create the energy it needs to operate. This provides instantaneous power although superchargers also use more fuel than a turbo which just uses exhaust gasses to operate.

Conclusion

In conclusion both a turbo and a supercharger aim to add more air to create more kinetic energy. The main difference between them is that a turbo runs off of exhaust gasses which make it more fuel effecient but also creates lag because of the time it takes for exhaust gasses to get to the turbo. A supercharger runs off a an engines serpentine belt and provides instantaneous power at the cost of more fuel. With the current era of turbos, turbo technology has aimed at creating less lag while supercharger technology has remained almost stagnant making the turbo the best of its class.
